About Thermal Recycling

Thermal recycling, such as pyrolysis and gasification, involves heating plastic waste in the absence of oxygen to break it down into fuels, gases, or raw materials. Unlike traditional recycling, it can handle mixed or contaminated plastics.

Preparation of new products from waste by using thermal recycling method

Thermal recycling, also known as thermal treatment or thermal conversion, is a solution for treating non-recyclable and non-reusable waste in an environmentally and economically friendly way. It reduces the volume and mass of the waste and inerts the hazardous components while generating thermal or electrical energy and minimizing pollutant emissions to air and water. Thermal recycling can involve the use of combustible packaging waste for the production of energy through direct incineration with or without other types of waste.


Types of Thermal Recycling Methods

  • Pyrolysis – Heating waste in the absence of oxygen to break it down into oils, gases, and char.
  • Gasification – Partial combustion of waste in a low-oxygen environment to produce syngas (a mix of carbon monoxide and hydrogen) used for energy production.
  • Incineration with Energy Recovery – Burning waste at high temperatures to generate heat and electricity.
  • Hydrothermal Treatment – Using high-pressure steam to break down waste into useful chemicals.

How Thermal Recycling Works

  • Collection & Sorting – Waste materials, especially non-recyclable plastics, are gathered.
  • Processing – The waste is subjected to high temperatures under controlled conditions.
  • Conversion – Depending on the method used, the waste turns into gases, oils, or raw materials.
  • Utilization – The byproducts are used for energy generation or as raw materials for new products.

2.preparation of paints by using plastics

Plastic paints offer several advantages, making them a popular choice for both residential and commercial applications. They are highly durable and resistant to wear and tear, ensuring long-lasting protection for walls and surfaces. Many plastic paints come with anti-fungal properties, preventing mold and algae growth, which is especially beneficial in humid environments. Moreover, eco-friendly, low-VOC options are available, making them a safer choice for both health and the environment. With a wide range of colors and finishes to choose from, plastic paints offer both functionality and aesthetic appeal, making them a versatile and practical option for modern spaces.

3.preparation of fuel

. What It Is: Thermal decomposition of plastic waste in the absence of oxygen to produce liquid fuel, gas, and char. 🔧 Basic Steps:

  1. Shred and dry plastic waste (LDPE, HDPE, PP).
  2. Heat in a pyrolysis reactor at 350–500°C without oxygen.
  3. Vapors condense into synthetic fuel (diesel or kerosene).
  4. Byproducts: Combustible gas (used for heating), and carbon char.
